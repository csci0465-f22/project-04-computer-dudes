Mental Health Across the United States

Making use of a choropleth to graph mental health across the United States the conclusion is drawn that North Dakota ranks among the highest in terms of best mental health while West Virginia ranks the lowest. This is interesting across two states which have similar population density. We take Williams County of North Dakota and McDowell county of West Virginia as a case example to convey a story of how social community and workplace experience shapes this phenomenon.

We use a broad data set of sociohealth statistics in order to represent these rather unorthodox or niche quality of life metrics.

McDowell County, WV and Williams County, ND at a Glance

An initial comparison of Williams Count and McDowell shows their similarity in many statistics one may initially point to for impacting mental health of an area. Both areas have similar unemployment rates, insufficient sleep, and obesity rates, leaving us to focus on other metrics.

Seeing that nothing from obesity, unemployment, or sleep stand out, notice the striking difference in both poverty and social community. Although social community may stand out less, this is just due to the scale of the visualization. In terms of ratio from one to another, Williams County dominates McDowell in this statistic. So, if the residents of both counties are employed at similar rates and in similar physical health, we point the finger at the quality of jobs and social community of each county as being the driving force in the disparity in mental health.

Social Life

Due to the extremely rural nature of each county, one may expect the social aspect of each to be similar. This however, is not the case. Residents of Williams County reported to have interacted with an average of 47 people per day. Although this number may be small compared to the likes of a city, it dwarves the 10 observed in McDowell County. Additionally, we cite Dunbar's Number which claims that a person can maintain 150 relationships as evidence that interacting with 47 people daily may very well provides a healthy social life. This claim is backed up by the strong mental health seen across Williams County.

Job Quality

In addition to social life we a see an impactful disparity across counties in job quality. Poverty may have been a tell tale sign of this, but the disparity goes much deeper that that. Beyond a higher median income, people in Williams County have on average a much shorter commute to work. Further, across both respective states as a whole, workers in North Dakota typically have a much higher satisfaction with their jobs. Putting all of this together, it comes as no surprise that the county where people maintain a healthy social life as well as enjoying a short commute to a well paying job they love each day performs so strongly in mental health measures.
