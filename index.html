What can regional data tell us about Mental Health in the US?

Plotting

Mental Health

by County

This choropleth graphs the number of average poor mental health days by county across the United States. Looking at the graph, we determined that West Virginia shows the highest concentration of larger values and North Dakota seems to rank the best. To get even more specific, we can analyze the most extreme cases in each state: Williams County (North Dakota) and McDowell County (West Virginia). We will use these counties as case examples to convey how social community and workplace experience statistics correlate with regional mental health. In terms of hard data, the average number of poor mental health days per month in Williams County is 3.33, compared to McDowell's 7.46.

To visualize these phenomenon, we're using a broad data set of sociohealth statistics to analyze various quality of life metrics as they relate to mental health.

County at a Glance

This radar chart visualizes a comparison of Williams County and McDowell County across five statistics that we considered potentially relevant to mental health. Looking at the grpah, we can see that both areas show similar rates for unemployment, insufficient sleep, and obesity, but that there are significant proportional differences in poverty and socialization rates.

Although the social score difference may be less initially stark, this is because both percentages are numerically smaller. In terms of ratio, Williams County dominates McDowell in this statistic. Given that the residents of both counties are employed at similar rates and that physical health differences are nonsignificant, we can point to job quality and the sociability of each community as a significant influence on the respective disparities in mental health.

Social Life

Due to the rural nature of each county, one may expect the socialiability scores of each to be similar. This however, is not the case. Residents of Williams County reported that they interact with an average of 47 people per day. And although this number may be small compared to the likes of a city, it dwarves the 10 observed in McDowell County. Additionally, we cite Dunbar's Number which claims that a person can maintain 150 relationships as evidence that interacting with 47 people daily may very well provides a healthy social life.

Job Quality

In addition to social life variability between the counties, we a see an impactful disparity in job quality. Poverty rate is a sure marker of this, but we can get more specific. Beyond a higher median income, people in Williams County have a much shorter (average) commute to work and workers in North Dakota typically have a much higher satisfaction with their jobs. Putting all of this together, it comes as no surprise that the county where people maintain healthy social lives paired with less draining work days and higher pay performs so much more strongly in mental health measures.
